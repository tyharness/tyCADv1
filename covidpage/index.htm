MEMS devices have become fairly common place since I first looked at the design of vibrating tuning forks used for measuring angular rate back in 90s (20th C.) 25 years later, every car has a dozen sensors (air bags, traction control etc.) and every smart phone has got 3 or 4 mems devices inside the case. "The detection of multiple target molecules in a small volume of sample has immediate relevance in the early detection of diseases, such as viruses of the type HIV and Herpes, which fall in the size of 100nm......The ability to tailor the surface properties with specific biomolecules can be used to create chemically functionalized MEMS/NEMS cantilever for study of physical chemistry, bio molecular biocompatibility and interactions.........The sample mass lowers the resonance frequency to a value of the order of few hundreds of Hz ......The mass of herpes virus is in the range of (54- 92)e+06 dalton....The maximum number of viruses that can attach in pit is accordingly 122.24e+06, corresponding to a mass of 18.64 ng while the minimum mass is 10.4 ng."
Let me just work through these numbers:
1 nm = 1 e-9m, 1 um = 1 e-6m,
1 dalton = 1.661e-27 kg
1ng = 1e-12kg
(range)Mass of virus:
mv0 = 54e6 Dalton = 8.969e-20kg
mv1 = 92e6 Dalton = 1.125e-19kg

Volume of the pit (quoted in the above paper) is Vp = 200um x 80um x 4um = 6.4e4 um^3
Diameter of a virus = 50nm
Volume of virus Vv=4/3piR^3 = 6.4e13 nm^3
nv = Vp/Vv = 122.231e6 the max number of virus
Therefore:
nv x mv0 = 122.231e6 x 8.969e-20kg / 1e-12 kg/ng = 11ng
nv x mv1 = 122.231e6 x 1.595e-19kg / 1e-12 kg/ng = 19.5ng
avg. mass mv = 15ng

therefore a sort of rough density, rho = mv/Vv = 15ng/6.4e13 nm^3 = 2.344e-13ng/nm^3

Please note all my maths need checking over. Moudgil and Swaminathan's nano scale cantilever beam has many natural frequencies and it's intersesting and how the addition of virus mass shifts the expected natural frequencies and then you can deduce the mass of the sample.



Label-free sensitive detection of influenza virus using PZT discs with a synthetic sialylglycopolymer receptor layer Alexander S. Erofeev, Petr V. Gorelkin, Dmitry V. Kolesov, Gleb A. Kiselev, Evgeniy V. Dubrovin and Igor V. Yaminsky Published:18 September 2019

"....label-free detection of Influenza A viruses using the first radial mode of oscillations of lead zirconate titanate (PZT) piezoelectric discs with a 2 mm radius and 100 μm thickness....discs are modified with a synthetic sialylglycopolymer receptor layer, and the coated discs are inserted in a flowing virus suspension.......detection of the virus is achieved by monitoring the disc radial mode resonance frequency shift. Piezo transducers...........viruses at concentrations below 10^5 virus particles per millilitre...experiments, the adsorption of virions from solution to the polymer receptor layer generated a positive shift in resonance frequency.... The interaction between virus particles and the receptor layer on the surface of PZT discs leads to an increased frequency of the radial oscillation mode. This increase may be due to intermolecular forces in the receptor layer, which can add additional strain to the resonator system. The use of the radial oscillation mode allows excluding much of the influence of media on the resonance properties of the sensor. We detected viruses at concentrations of 10 5 virions ml −1 and demonstrated meaningful differences between the frequency shifts for concentrations of 0, 10^5 , 10^6 and 10^7 virions ml^−1 ."

Using MEMS to build a virus testing lab. on-a-chip:

This is my attempt at designing a MEMS device but please note it is only, at best, an idealistic model and there's an awful lot of engineering needed to roll out a MEMS device in the real world. I'm not affiliated with any business or actively working in the field of mems unless you want to give me a challenge.

I have a little experience in designing resonant frequency mems devices therefore could we detect a small amount of virus mass with a suspended mass spring flexure device etched into silicon and will it be sensitive enough to find a very small change in mass?? Why should we use a suspended mass and long cantilever beam/spring system: Well you get a good linearity response along with a low structural damping coefficient. As a bench mark I wanted to take a look at a single degree of freedom (DOF) model.



mems1b.pdf
mems1cmaths.pdf

But I can't be sure of the real damping coefficient without experimental data which could make the change in frequency un-measurable because the q factor maybe poor. Another pitfall will be the coupling of other (spurious) modes by accident such as a torsional motion and or out-of-plane resonance, but this can be easily designed out -- Now this I can do....

My idea is simply a spring mass damper(smd) described by Frahm 1909. It's now a 2 DOF problem and I believe I can shift the 2 natural frequencies apart. You then tune the smd to match the exciting force's F0 frequency and then all the displacement of the main system will stop, X1=0 and X2 = -F0/k2. So rather than using the second mass as a damper i'm using it in a tuning sense. You could use the smd device in several ways:
1) Add a controlled known mass equal to sample plus virus so they are equal so X1=0, 2) Active electro-thermal tuning of k2 using DC current until X1=0 and read off the deflection, X2 with capacitive sensing comb and or the DC current, and 3)If the device is sensitive enough (i.e a sharp q factor) then the mass ratio (m1+mv / m2) will have calculatable frequency difference w2-w1 which might just indicate if the expected number of virons are present in the real sample.

Please note this is just a proposal for discussion and not a proven or approved design in anyway.
